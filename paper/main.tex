\begin{abstract}
% Write last. Target 150 words. Fill in X, Y, Z from results.
We present a physics-informed neural network (PINN) surrogate of FSRU
regasification that treats LNG composition as a dynamic state, and couple it
to day-ahead electricity price signals to compute economically optimal
send-out schedules. Using the Independence FSRU in Klaip\polhk eda as a
reference and Lithuanian (LT) ENTSO-E day-ahead prices, we show that
composition-aware dispatch yields \textbf{X\%} cost reduction over
composition-blind baselines across a multi-year backtest, and that the PINN
surrogate matches a CoolProp reference simulator to within \textbf{Y\%} on
energy balance residuals while running \textbf{$\sim$Z$\times$} faster.
\end{abstract}
